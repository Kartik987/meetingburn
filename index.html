The True Cost of Corporate Meetings

Time is money, but in modern corporate environments, time is often wasted. Our free Meeting Burn Rate Calculator helps managers, Scrum Masters, and team leads visualize exactly how much capital is being drained during lengthy internal calls.

Whether you are sitting through a bloated Agile sprint planning session, a drawn-out QA synchronization, or a metrics management review that could have been an email, seeing the dollars tick up in real-time is the fastest way to enforce efficient communication.

How the Burn Rate is Calculated

This web utility uses a standard baseline for corporate calculation. We assume a standard 2,080-hour working year (40 hours a week for 52 weeks). By dividing the average annual salary by 2,080, we find the hourly rate. We then break that down to a per-second rate and multiply it by the number of attendees in the room.

Because this tool operates entirely on your browser using client-side processing, no data regarding your team's salary or headcount is ever stored or saved on a database.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I track meeting costs?

Tracking meeting costs provides a visual, tangible metric for time management. When a team realizes a single daily standup costs the company ₹400 a week, they are more likely to stay on topic, reduce attendee lists, and move minor updates to text-based channels.

How can I reduce my meeting burn rate?

The easiest method is to strictly limit the attendee list to active contributors. Secondary stakeholders should receive a written summary rather than an invite. Adopting asynchronous tools for standard updates can completely eliminate the need for certain recurring calendar blocks.
